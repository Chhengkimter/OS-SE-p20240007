--- Zombie and Orphan Cleanup Notes ---
Q: How are orphans cleaned up? A: Orphans are adopted by a 'subreaper' process, usually PID 1 (init or systemd). This new parent monitors the process and reaps its exit status once it eventually finishes execution.
Q: How are zombies cleaned up? A: Zombies are cleaned up when their parent process executes a wait() or waitpid() system call. This allows the kernel to release the remaining process table entry.
Q: Can you kill a zombie with 'kill -9'? Why or why not? A: No, you cannot kill a zombie because it is already dead. A zombie is simply a record of a terminated process. To remove it, you must either wait for the parent to reap it or kill the parent process so that PID 1 can adopt and clean it up.
